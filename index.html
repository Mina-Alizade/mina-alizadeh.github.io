I study the late-stage evolution of stars in the Triangulum Galaxy (M33) through near-infrared photometric monitoring. My research centres on long-period variable stars — AGB stars on the verge of shedding their envelopes — and their role in shaping the chemical and dust budget of galaxies.

Areas of Focus

Research

🔭

Variable Stars & AGB Evolution

Long-period variable (LPV) and AGB stars in M33, studied through 15-year UKIRT multi-epoch NIR photometry spanning 2003–2018. Variability characterisation using GLS periodograms, RMS-based detection, and Gaussian Process modelling.

🌌

Stellar Populations in M33

Constructing a homogenised multi-instrument catalogue of 847 variable stars across UIST, UFTI, WFCAM, and UHS epochs. PSF photometry with Photutils, cross-matched against OGLE, ASAS-SN, Spitzer, and JWST archives.

💨

Dust & Stellar Winds

Characterising mass-loss rates and dusty outflows from evolved stars across M33's galactic disk. Colour diagnostics using JWST and Spitzer mid-infrared photometry to trace dust production as a function of stellar luminosity and pulsation amplitude.
